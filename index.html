Lanthanoid Contraction: Steady decrease in size of \(M^{3+}\) ions from Ce to Lu. Impact: Zr/Hf have identical sizes!
Magnetic & Catalytic
Formula:\(\mu = \sqrt{n(n+2)}\) BM
⭐ n = Number of unpaired electrons. More 'n' = More magnetic.
Catalysis: TM are good catalysts due to: 1. Variable OS, 2. Large Surface Area.
K2Cr2O7 & KMnO4 High Yields
K2Cr2O7: Chromite ore \(\to\) Sodium Chromate (Yellow) \(\to\) Sodium Dichromate (Orange) \(\to\) Potassium Dichromate.
KMnO4: Pyrolusite (\(MnO_2\)) \(\xrightarrow{KOH, O_2}\) \(K_2MnO_4\) (Green) \(\xrightarrow{H^+}\) \(KMnO_4\) (Purple).
IUPAC Naming Master
  • Ligands: Ammine, Aqua, Carbonyl, Nitrosyl (Neutral).
  • Alphabetic order: Always name ligands alphabetically.
  • Metal Name: 'ate' suffix if complex is negative (e.g., Cobaltate).
Example: \(K_3[Fe(CN)_6]\) \(\to\) Potassium hexacyanidoferrate(III).
Bonding (VBT & CFT)
Hybrid:\(sp^3, dsp^2, d^2sp^3\)
Spectrochemical Series: \(CO > CN^- > en > NH_3\) (Strong Field). Strong ligands cause pairing.
CFSE: \(\Delta_o\) (Octahedral) vs \(\Delta_t\) (Tetrahedral). \(\Delta_t = \frac{4}{9} \Delta_o\)

Why? (Top Board Questions)
Q: Why is \(Cu^{2+}\) more stable than \(Cu^+\) in aqueous solution?
A: Due to much higher hydration enthalpy of \(Cu^{2+}\).
Q: Why do TM show variable oxidation states?
A: Because the energy gap between (n-1)d and ns orbitals is very small.
Q: Why do Coordination compounds show isomerism?
A: Due to different spatial arrangements of ligands (Geometrical/Optical).
